7. Observations & Considerations

  • Roll is the dominant motion axis. Peak roll amplitude (±6.5°) is 4× the pitch amplitude (±1.5°). This confirms roll stabilization is the correct focus — a gyro stabilizer addresses exactly this.
  • Moderate conditions produced significant roll. At only Beaufort 3–4 with 0.5 m waves, the vessel already reaches ±6.5° roll. In higher sea states during survey operations, roll amplitudes will increase substantially.
  • Fast roll period (~4.0 s). Typical for a catamaran hull form. This is favourable for gyro stabilization — shorter periods mean higher roll rates, which generate greater precession and thus more anti-roll torque from the gyroscopic effect. The SG150's hydraulic braking system is dynamically adjusted by its control unit to match the roll period.
  • Mean trim is +1.07° bow-up. Consistent and stable throughout the recording. This is a static trim condition, not a dynamic concern for gyro sizing.
  • Roll rates reach ±10°/s in peaks. These angular velocities drive the precession demand on the gyro. The SG150 control system dynamically adjusts hydraulic braking to regulate precession — high roll rates are the working condition, not a limitation.
  • Data quality is excellent. All 19 493 samples have status "Normal" (status code 2) across all four status fields (attitude, heading, heave, velocity). RTK GPS with 12 satellites and HDOP 0.8 — this is survey-grade data.
  • Single sea state recorded. This log represents one 32-minute window at Beaufort 3–4. For complete gyro sizing, additional recordings at higher sea states, different headings relative to wave direction, and during DP operations would strengthen the analysis.

8. Open Questions for Gyro Evaluation

  • What is Northern Gale's displacement and GM (metacentric height)? Required for calculating whether one SG150 provides sufficient anti-roll moment, or if multiple units are needed.
  • What is the maximum acceptable roll during survey operations? The current unstabilized ±6.5° at Bft 3–4 may be the baseline for a reduction target (e.g. 50–70% reduction).
  • Where on the vessel can 1.96 tonnes (per unit) be accommodated? The SG150 requires 1.36×1.41×1.02 m footprint, 380V 3-phase AC, 24V DC, and sea water cooling at 35–55 LPM.
  • Is the existing 380V supply sufficient for the 6 kW spool-up demand, given the hybrid propulsion conversion is also in progress?
  • Additional sea-trial logs at Bft 5+ and beam sea conditions would be valuable for worst-case gyro sizing.
