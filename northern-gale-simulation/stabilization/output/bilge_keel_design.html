Northern Gale — Bilge Keel Design

Hull H209 · 26m High-Speed Catamaran · Passive Roll Stabilisation
OPTION B: 200mm × 10m × 2 KEELS
Design Overview
Bilge keels monterade på ytterbilgen av varje skrov. Passiv lösning utan effektbehov — energin absorberas genom hydrodynamiskt motstånd (vortex shedding) vid rollrörelse. Katamaran-geometrin ger en unik fördel: hävarmen (4.80m) är 2–3× längre än monoskrov, vilket ger ~4× effektivare dämpning per areaenhet.

Slutsats: Bilge keels ger jämförbar rollreduktion som aktiva fenor — men utan el, utan rörliga delar, utan internt utrymme, och till en bråkdel av kostnaden. Den enda nackdelen är begränsad effekt vid noll fart, vilket sällan förekommer under survey-operationer. Om zero-speed stabilisering blir kritiskt i framtiden kan bilge keels kombineras med ett gyro (hybrid-approach).

2. Key Assumptions

Varje antagande är klassificerat efter källa. MEASURED = direkt från Seapath-data. ESTIMATED = ingenjörsbedömning. LITERATURE = värde från publicerad referens. DERIVED = beräknat från andra värden.

Measured Troll ≈ 3.8 s — Natural roll period, uppskattad från Seapath Log 1 och Log 2 peak-frekvensanalys. Bör verifieras med formellt free roll decay test.
Estimated KG = 2.8 m — Tyngdpunktshöjd ovan baslinje. Baserat på typiskt KG/D-ratio (0.65–0.75) för denna typ av aluminium-katamaran med däckshus. Bör hämtas från stabilitetsbok.
Literature k44 = 0.35 × B = 3.85 m — Rolltröghetradie. 0.35 × B rekommenderas av ITTC (2011) för katamaraner. Monoskrov använder typiskt 0.35–0.45 × B.
Derived GM = 4.13 m — Beräknat från Troll och k44. Högt GM är förväntat för katamaran med bred hullspacing. Bekräftar den stela, snäppiga rullkaraktäristiken.
Estimated ζbare = 0.020 — Bare hull damping ratio (dimensionslös). Typiskt 0.015–0.03 för katamaraner utan appendix. Monoskrov: 0.03–0.08. Bör verifieras med free roll decay.
Literature A44 = 0.15 × I44 — Added mass moment of inertia i roll, typiskt 10–20% av I44. 15% valt som mittenvärde. Källa: Himeno (1981), ITTC (2011).
Estimated BK position: y=4.16m, z=0.4m — Bilge keel placerad vid ytterbilgövergång, 0.4m ovan baslinje. Uppskattat från midship section-ritningen och deadrise-vinkeln 23.6°.
Literature f(σ) = 1.0 — Ikedas sektionskoefficientfunktion. σ ≈ 0.7 för katamaranens skrovsektion ger f = 1 + 0.3 × exp(-160 × (1-0.7)) ≈ 1.0000. Källa: Ikeda (1978e).
Estimated Cp integral ≈ 0.10 — Tryckkoefficient-integral för bilge keel-hull interaction (BBKH). Förenklad; fullständig beräkning kräver integrering över skrovets girth.
Measured Sjöförhållanden — Bft 3–4 (mild) och Bft 5–6 (rough) baserat på besättningens uppskattning. Ej instrumentellt verifierad våghöjd.

3. Step 1 — Roll Dynamics

1
Roll angular frequency from measured period
ω = 2π / Troll = 2π / 3.8 = 1.653 rad/s
2
Metacentric height from roll period (inverted natural frequency relation)
Troll = 2π × k44 / √(g × GM) Solving for GM: GM = (2π × k44 / Troll)² / g = (2π × 3.85 / 3.8)² / 9.81 = (6.367)² / 9.81 = 40.54 / 9.81 = 4.13 m
3
Mass moment of inertia in roll
I44 = Δ × k44² = 121,900 × 3.85² = 121,900 × 14.8225 = 1,806,863 kg·m² A44 = 0.15 × I44 = 0.15 × 1,806,863 = 271,029 kg·m² Itotal = I44 + A44 = 1,806,863 + 271,029 = 2,077,892 kg·m²
4
Roll restoring moment coefficient
Croll = Δ × g × GM = 121,900 × 9.81 × 4.13 = 4,939,895 N·m/rad
5
Bare hull damping coefficients
B44,crit = 2 × Itotal × ω = 2 × 2,077,892 × 1.653 = 6,871,464 N·m·s/rad B44,bare = 2 × ζbare × Itotal × ω = 2 × 0.020 × 2,077,892 × 1.653 = 137,429 N·m·s/rad

4. Step 2 — Bilge Keel Geometry

6
Bilge keel position (outer bilge of each hull)
yBK = yhull center + Bhull/2 = 3.00 + 2.32/2 = 4.16 m from CL zBK = 0.40 m above baseline (at bilge turn, estimated from deadrise)
7
Lever arm from roll axis (through G) to bilge keel
r = √(yBK² + (KG - zBK)²) = √(4.16² + (2.80 - 0.40)²) = √(17.306 + 5.760) = √23.066 = 4.80 m Compare monohull ~25m: r ≈ 1.5–2.5 m Catamaran advantage: r² = 23.04 m² vs monohull r² ≈ 4–6 m² → Damping moment scales with r², giving ~4–6× more effective per m² BK area
8
Bilge keel design dimensions (Option B)
bBK = 0.200 m (span / protrusion from hull) LBK = 10.0 m (length, = 42% of LWL) n = 2 (number of keels, outer bilge each hull) Area per keel: 0.200 × 10.0 = 2.00 m² Total area: 2 × 2.00 = 4.00 m² bBK/Bhull = 0.200 / 2.32 = 8.6% (typical range: 3–6% for large ships)

5. Step 3 — Ikeda Bilge Keel Damping Components

Baserat på Ikeda et al. (1978a-e), formulerat av Bassler & Reed (2009). Bilge keel-dämpningen består av tre komponenter: BBKN (normalkraft), BBKH (hull interaction), BBKW (wave-making, försumbar).

9
Bilge keel normal force component at zero speed (Bassler & Reed Eq. 6)
Formula: Ikeda (1978a), Bassler & Reed (2009) Eq. 6 BBKN0 = (8/3π) × ρ × r² × bBK² × ω × f² × [22.5/(π×f) + 2.4 × (r×φ/bBK)] Where: ρ = 1025 kg/m³ (seawater density) r = 4.80 m (lever arm, roll axis → BK) bBK = 0.200 m (bilge keel span) ω = 1.653 rad/s (roll angular frequency) f = 1.0000 (section coefficient function, f(σ=0.7)) φ = roll amplitude (radians, varies by condition) Note: BBKN0 is per unit length. Multiply by LBK × (n/2) for total.
10
Worked example: Rough conditions (φ = 7.0° = 0.1222 rad)
Pre-compute constant terms: 8/(3π) = 0.8488 Term 1: viscous drag 22.5/(π × f) = 22.5/(π × 1.0) = 7.162 Term 2: amplitude-dependent 2.4 × r × φ / bBK = 2.4 × 4.80 × 0.1222 / 0.200 = 7.039 Combined bracket: [7.162 + 7.039] = 14.201 BBKN0 per unit length: = 0.8488 × 1025 × 4.80² × 0.200² × 1.653 × 1.0² × 14.201 = 0.8488 × 1025 × 23.04 × 0.04 × 1.653 × 14.201 = 0.8488 × 1025 × 23.04 × 0.04 × 23.47 = 0.8488 × 1025 × 21.63 = 0.8488 × 22,172 = 18,822 N·m·s/rad per meter length Total BBKN0 (× LBK × n/2 pairs): = 18,822 × 10.0 × 1 = 188,220 N·m·s/rad (Script output: 188,515 — difference due to rounding in manual calc)
11
Bilge keel-hull interaction component (Bassler & Reed Eq. 8)
Formula: Ikeda (1978b), Bassler & Reed (2009) Eq. 8 BBKH = (4/3π) × r² × d⁴ × f² × ω × φ × ∫Cp × l dG Simplified (using Cp integral ≈ 0.10): = (4/3π) × ρ × r² × T⁴ × f² × ω × φ × Cp,int × LBK × (n/2) = 0.4244 × 1025 × 23.04 × 1.55⁴ × 1.0 × 1.653 × 0.1222 × 0.10 × 10.0 × 1 = 0.4244 × 1025 × 23.04 × 5.768 × 1.653 × 0.1222 × 0.10 × 10.0 = ~11,700 N·m·s/rad BBKH ≈ 6% of BBKN0 — as expected, hull interaction is secondary.
12
Total bilge keel damping and resulting damping ratio
BBK = BBKN + BBKH = 188,515 + 11,700 = 200,214 N·m·s/rad B44,total = B44,bare + BBK = 137,429 + 200,214 = 337,644 N·m·s/rad ζtotal = B44,total / B44,crit = 337,644 / 6,871,464 = 0.049 Damping increase: 0.049 / 0.020 = 2.5× over bare hull

6. Step 4 — Roll Reduction Estimate

13
Roll Reduction Ratio (RRR) — near-resonance approximation
At or near natural frequency, roll amplitude is inversely proportional to damping: φmax ∝ 1/(2ζ) Therefore: RRR = 1 - ζbare / ζtotal = 1 - 0.020 / 0.049 = 1 - 0.408 = 0.593 = 59.3% (at φ=7° rough conditions) Note: This is a theoretical maximum at resonance. Real-world conditions include off-resonance waves → lower effective reduction. Calibrated range: 0.8 × RRR to 1.0 × RRR, capped at 55%. → Reported range: 47% – 55% (midpoint 51%)

7. Step 5 — Speed Penalty

15
Wetted surface estimation (per hull)
Shull = LWL × (2T + Bhull) × Cws = 23.99 × (2×1.55 + 2.32) × 0.72 = 23.99 × 5.42 × 0.72 = 93.6 m² per hull Stotal = 2 × 93.6 = 187.2 m² Cws = 0.72 wetted surface coefficient (estimated for catamaran hull form)
16
Added wetted surface from bilge keels (Option B)
Both sides of flat plate keel: SBK = 2 × bBK × LBK × n = 2 × 0.200 × 10.0 × 2 = 8.0 m² ΔS/S = 8.0 / 187.2 = 4.3%
17
Resistance increase at 10 kn (ITTC-57 friction line)
Speed: V = 10 kn = 5.144 m/s Reynolds number: Re = V × LWL / ν = 5.144 × 23.99 / 1.19×10⁻⁶ = 1.037 × 10⁸ ITTC-57 friction coefficient: Cf = 0.075 / (log₁₀(Re) - 2)² = 0.075 / (8.016 - 2)² = 0.075 / 36.19 = 0.00207 Frictional resistance increase (friction ≈ 40% of total at semi-displacement): ΔRfriction = ΔS/S × 0.40 = 4.3% × 0.40 = 1.7% Form drag (bilge keel pressure drag at survey speed, well-aligned): Cd,form = 0.005 (aligned with streamlines) ΔRform ≈ Cd,form × ρ × ABK × V / (Cf × ρ × Stotal × V) ≈ 1.0% Total: ΔRtotal ≈ 1.7% + 1.0% = ~2.7% at 10 kn Speed loss (at constant power): ΔV/V ≈ ΔR/(2+n), n≈0 for displacement → ~1.3% speed loss = ~0.13 kn at 10 kn

8. Step 6 — Weight Calculation

18
Material weight (5083 H321: ρ = 2660 kg/m³)
Bilge keel plates (2 × 200mm × 10m × 10mm): Wplate = 2 × 0.200 × 10.0 × 0.010 × 2660 = 106.4 kg Ground bar / doubler (2 × 50mm × 10m × 8mm): Wground = 2 × 0.050 × 10.0 × 0.008 × 2660 = 21.3 kg Brackets (10 pcs × ~0.3 kg each): Wbrackets = 10 × 0.3 = 3.0 kg Taper ends (4 pcs × ~0.5 kg each): Wtaper = 4 × 0.5 = 2.0 kg Wtotal = 106.4 + 21.3 + 3.0 + 2.0 = 132.7 kg Percentage of displacement: 132.7 / 121,900 = 0.109% → Negligible impact on stability, trim, and speed

9. Step 7 — Lateral Acceleration Prediction

19
Scaling of lateral acceleration with roll reduction
Lateral acceleration at height h above roll center: alat = g × sin(φ) + φ̈ × h ≈ g × φ + ω² × φ × h (small angle) Since alat scales linearly with φ for small angles: alat,new = alat,baseline × (1 - RRR) Wheelhouse (rough conditions), midpoint estimate (RRR = 51%): alat,new = 0.060 × (1 - 0.51) = 0.060 × 0.49 = 0.029g AFS Insatsvärde: 0.051g → 0.029g < 0.051g → COMPLIANT ✓ Range with uncertainty (47%–55%): Low: 0.060 × 0.53 = 0.032g (still below 0.051g ✓) Mid: 0.060 × 0.49 = 0.029g (well below ✓) High: 0.060 × 0.45 = 0.027g (well below ✓)

12. Model Limitations

Limitation Ikeda method developed for monohulls. Original validation data is for conventional cargo ships (Cb 0.56–0.85). Extension to catamaran hull forms is approximative. The lever arm calculation is straightforward, but hull interaction effects (BBKH) and wave-making effects (BBKW) may differ significantly.
Limitation Linear superposition assumption. Roll damping is inherently nonlinear (drag ∝ velocity²). The equivalent linearization (Fourier averaging) used here is accurate for single-frequency excitation but less precise for broadband wave spectra.
Limitation No frequency weighting applied. AFS compliance assessment uses unweighted RMS acceleration. ISO 2631-1 requires frequency-weighted acceleration (Wd for lateral). The assessment is therefore indicative, not formally compliant.
Limitation Sea state estimated, not measured. Wave conditions based on Beaufort scale estimates by crew. No wave buoy or radar wave measurement available. Actual Hs unknown.
Limitation Speed penalty is simplified. Uses ITTC-57 friction line and estimated form drag. Does not account for spray, wave-making interaction, or flow separation at high Froude numbers. CFD analysis would provide better accuracy.
Limitation No forward speed effect on BK damping. Eq. 7 (Bassler & Reed) adds a forward speed term to BBKN. This was omitted as Northern Gale primarily operates at low speed (7–10 kn) where the zero-speed term dominates. At transit (14 kn), BK effectiveness may be slightly higher than predicted.
